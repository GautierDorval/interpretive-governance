Interpretive Governance is a doctrinal approach to controlling what machine systems are permitted to interpret and say in public. The goal is bounded interpretation, not maximal output.

This public site can define boundaries, precedence, refusal conditions, and canonical references. Operational execution remains private and mandate-bound.

The hard public rule

1) Interpret
Only within declared scope and provenance.
2) Authorize
Only answer when response-legitimacy conditions are satisfied.
3) Constrain
Keep the answer inside declared output constraints.
4) Refuse
When legitimacy fails, the correct behavior is clarification, abstention, or legitimate non-response.
